Welcome to DICT

The Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT) is dedicated to advancing digital infrastructure, enabling innovation, and promoting sustainable development through technology. We strive to build a digitally inclusive society for all.

Our Mission

“DICT of the people and for the people."

The Department of Information and Communications Technology commits to:

•Provide every Filipino access to vital ICT infostructure and services.

•Ensure sustainable growth of Philippine ICT-enabled industries resulting to creation of more jobs.

•Establish a One Digitized Government, One Nation.

•Support the administration in fully achieving its goals.

•Be the enabler, innovator, achiever and leader in pushing the country’s development and transition towards a world-class digital economy.

Our Vision

“An innovative, safe and happy nation that thrives through and is enabled by Information and Communications Technology.”

DICT aspires for the Philippines to develop and flourish through innovation and constant development of ICT in the pursuit of a progressive, safe, secured, contented and happy Filipino nation.

Key Initiatives

Free Wi-Fi for All

This program provides free internet access in public spaces like parks, schools, and government offices. It ensures internet accessibility in underserved areas, bridging the digital divide and helping citizens access education, government services, and information.

Cybersecurity Bureau

The Cybersecurity Bureau develops policies to protect the country’s digital infrastructure from cyber threats. It responds to cyberattacks, provides security awareness training, and strengthens the cybersecurity capabilities of government agencies and businesses to safeguard national data and systems.

ICT Literacy and Competency Development Bureau (ILCDB)

The ILCDB focuses on improving digital literacy across the Philippines through education and training programs. It promotes the National ICT Competency Framework (NICF) to guide learning and helps local governments implement ICT literacy programs to prepare Filipinos for the digital economy.

About DICT

The Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT) is the central authority in the implementation of policies and initiatives in ICT. Our vision is to create a digitally empowered society through technology and innovation.

Our History

Founded in May 23, 2016, DICT was established to unify the country's ICT services under one umbrella. Over the years, we’ve contributed to numerous technological advancements and digital infrastructure development across the nation.

Our Core Objectives

  • Improve national ICT infrastructure and connectivity
  • Support e-governance initiatives for easier public access to services
  • Develop a safe and secure digital environment for all citizens
  • Bridge the digital divide in underserved and rural areas

Our Services

We offer a wide range of services aimed at improving digital access and promoting a digitally inclusive society. Below are some of our key offerings that help empower communities through technology.

ICT Literacy and Competency Development Bureau

The ICT Literacy and Competency Development Bureau is focused on improving the digital skills of Filipinos, particularly through educational and training programs. It aims to enhance the ICT competencies of individuals to help them adapt to the fast-evolving digital world. The bureau provides resources and frameworks such as the National ICT Competency Framework (NICF) to guide learning and training initiatives. It also supports local government units (LGUs) in implementing ICT literacy programs that empower citizens to actively participate in the digital economy. By equipping Filipinos with these necessary skills, the bureau helps to prepare the workforce for the demands of the digital age.

ICT Industry Development Bureau

The ICT Industry Development Bureau works to nurture and grow the ICT sector in the Philippines. It focuses on enhancing the competitiveness of local ICT businesses and promoting the Philippines as a global player in the digital economy. Through investment promotion programs, it encourages both local and foreign investments in ICT and supports innovation and research in the industry. The bureau also develops policies and frameworks that help ICT businesses thrive, ensuring that the industry adapts to the latest technological trends. Additionally, it provides capacity-building programs to strengthen the sector’s workforce and support economic growth through technology.

Digital Transformation Center

The Digital Transformation Center is responsible for helping both government agencies and private organizations adopt digital technologies to improve their operations. It provides advisory services and develops frameworks for integrating digital solutions into various sectors. The center promotes e-government services, enabling the public sector to offer more efficient and transparent services to citizens. It also offers training programs for government employees to enhance their digital skills and ensure effective use of digital tools in public service. In addition, the center supports the research and development of new technologies to promote innovation and digital transformation across the country.

Free Wi-Fi for All

The Free Wi-Fi for All program aims to provide accessible internet connectivity to Filipinos in public spaces such as parks, government offices, schools, and transportation hubs. The initiative helps bridge the digital divide by ensuring that underserved and remote areas have access to the internet, allowing citizens to access online education, government services, and other essential information. By providing free Wi-Fi, the government enables greater social inclusion and supports the growth of digital literacy. This program also promotes the use of digital tools in daily life and encourages Filipinos to participate more actively in the digital economy.

Philippine National Key Infrastructure

The Philippine National Key Infrastructure (PNKI) initiative focuses on safeguarding the country’s critical infrastructures, such as telecommunications, transportation, energy, and financial systems. The PNKI aims to protect these essential services from both physical and cyber threats, ensuring their continued operation even in the face of disruptions. The program includes developing and implementing security measures and protocols to defend national infrastructure from cyberattacks and other vulnerabilities. By enhancing the resilience of these infrastructures, the PNKI ensures the stability of the nation’s economy and the security of its citizens.

Cybersecurity Bureau

The Cybersecurity Bureau plays a crucial role in safeguarding the Philippines from cyber threats. It is responsible for creating and enforcing cybersecurity policies that protect the country's digital infrastructure. The bureau focuses on ensuring the security of government data and critical sectors such as banking, healthcare, and telecommunications. It provides incident response to cyberattacks, helping both public and private organizations mitigate damage during breaches. The bureau also promotes cybersecurity awareness and provides capacity-building programs to enhance the skills of government personnel, businesses, and the general public. By doing so, it helps protect against data theft, fraud, and cybercrimes.

Integrated Business Permits and Licensing System

The Integrated Business Permits and Licensing System (IBPLS) is an automated platform designed to streamline the process of securing business permits and licenses. By digitizing this process, the system reduces bureaucracy and minimizes the time and effort required for entrepreneurs to start and operate businesses. The IBPLS integrates various government departments, enabling businesses to apply for, pay, and track permits and licenses all in one system. This initiative is part of the government's efforts to create a more business-friendly environment by promoting ease of doing business and improving transparency in government services.

Vaccine Information Management System

The Vaccine Information Management System (VIMS) is a crucial platform for managing and tracking the COVID-19 vaccination rollout in the Philippines. The system helps monitor vaccine distribution, administration, and reporting across the country, ensuring that vaccines are delivered to the right locations and populations. It also provides real-time data on vaccination progress, which aids in decision-making and planning. VIMS is used to issue digital vaccination certificates for individuals who have been vaccinated, allowing them to travel and participate in other activities that require proof of vaccination. It is essential for the government’s vaccination strategy, helping to ensure an organized and efficient vaccination process.

Barangay Records Automation & Management System

The Barangay Records Automation & Management System (BRAMS) is designed to modernize the way barangays manage their records. This system automates the collection, storage, and retrieval of vital documents such as resident information, permits, clearances, and other official records. By digitizing these processes, BRAMS ensures that barangays can offer quicker, more efficient services to citizens. The system also promotes transparency and reduces the risk of record loss or mismanagement. It integrates with other government systems, allowing for better coordination between local and national government agencies. Through BRAMS, barangays can improve their administrative functions and enhance service delivery to their communities.
